## 💡 **Usage Guidelines**

### **For Service Matching:**
- Match complexity to client experience level
- Consider technical requirements vs. available expertise
- Assess risk tolerance vs. service risk level
- Balance time investment with client availability

### **For Staff Assignment:**
- Assign simple services to junior staff
- Reserve complex services for senior stylists
- Match expertise level to service complexity
- Ensure proper supervision for learning

### **For Client Education:**
- Explain complexity level during consultation
- Set realistic expectations for results
- Discuss time and maintenance requirements
- Prepare clients for service experience

### **For Business Operations:**
- Price services according to complexity
- Schedule appropriate time for complexity level
- Ensure proper tools/equipment availability
- Plan staff development around complexity needs

### **For Quality Control:**
- Monitor results by complexity level
- Track success rates for different complexities
- Identify training needs by complexity gaps
- Maintain standards across complexity levels

**These complexity tags ensure appropriate service matching while maintaining clear, simple categorization.**
